## Executive Summary

### The Convergence Thesis

We are witnessing the largest infrastructure buildout in 50 years, driven by **four converging megatrends**:

1. **AI Compute Scaling** demands exponentially more power, cooling, and specialized chips
2. **Energy Infrastructure** must triple capacity while shifting to decentralized, reliable sources (nuclear renaissance)
3. **Crypto/Blockchain** requires massive computational infrastructure, creating demand for semiconductors and energy
4. **Robotics** enables automation of every physical process, multiplying demand for AI chips and energy efficiency

These aren't separate trends — they're **deeply interconnected**. The companies winning at the intersection of 2+ trends are asymmetrically positioned.

## Section 7: Risk Factors & Headwinds

### Geopolitical Risks
- **Taiwan exposure:** TSMC, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing. US-China tensions could disrupt supply.
- **Uranium supply:** Sanctions on Russia; US/Canada dependency on supply chain security.
- **Europe energy:** Germany's energy policy shift affecting Siemens, Infineon, other German firms.
- **Export controls:** US restrictions on selling chips to China amplifying competition/duplication.

### Technology Risks
- **Alternative compute architectures:** Quantum computing could make classical semiconductors obsolete (long-term, low probability near-term).
- **Custom chips eroding NVIDIA margins:** Hyperscalers (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA) reducing GPU demand.
- **Crypto regulation:** Harsh regulations could collapse mining demand and energy requirements.

### Market Risks
- **Valuation saturation:** NVIDIA trading at ~60x forward P/E; TSMC at ~30x. Any disappointment = sharp pullbacks.
- **Economic recession:** Semiconductor cycles are historically volatile; a downturn hits capex hard.
- **Energy price volatility:** Uranium, lithium, oil prices drive profitability. Commodity cycles are unpredictable.
